1. Musical Notes

Musical notes represent the pitch and duration of a sound in music. The standard notes in music are:

  • A, B, C, D, E, F, G (The natural notes)
  • Sharp (#) and Flat (b) are used to modify the pitch.
  • For example, C# is one semitone higher than C, and Bb is one semitone lower than B.

2. Time Signatures

A time signature indicates the number of beats in each measure (bar) of music. The most common time signatures are:

  • 4/4: Four beats per measure, and each beat is a quarter note.
  • 3/4: Three beats per measure, and each beat is a quarter note.
  • 6/8: Six beats per measure, and each beat is an eighth note.

Time signatures help musicians to understand the rhythm of the music and how to count each beat.

3. Chords

A chord is a group of notes played together. The most basic types of chords are:

  • Major Chords: A chord consisting of a root note, major third, and perfect fifth. (E.g., C Major = C, E, G)
  • Minor Chords: A chord consisting of a root note, minor third, and perfect fifth. (E.g., A Minor = A, C, E)
  • Seventh Chords: A chord consisting of a root note, major third, perfect fifth, and a seventh note. (E.g., C7 = C, E, G, Bb)

4. Scales

A scale is a series of notes ordered by pitch. The two most common types of scales are:

  • Major Scale: A scale that follows a specific pattern of whole and half steps (W-W-H-W-W-W-H). (E.g., C Major: C, D, E, F, G, A, B, C)
  • Minor Scale: A scale that has a more somber tone, often with a different pattern of whole and half steps. (E.g., A Minor: A, B, C, D, E, F, G, A)

5. Key Signatures
